Batman Three Jokers


Batman Three Jokers: "Batman: Three Jokers" is a comic book limited series published by DC Comics in 2020, written by Geoff Johns and illustrated by Jason Fabok. The story revolves around the revelation that there are three different individuals who have taken on the identity of the Joker throughout Batman's career. Here's a brief overview of the plot without diving into spoilers: 1. **Premise**: The story begins with Batman realizing that there are multiple Jokers, each with distinct characteristics and methods. This discovery shakes Batman as he tries to unravel the mystery behind these versions of his arch-nemesis. 2. **Characters**: Apart from Batman, the main characters include the three Jokers themselves, each representing a different era or aspect of the Joker persona. The story explores their motivations, origins, and relationships with Batman and other key characters like Batgirl and Red Hood. 3. **Themes**: The series delves into themes of identity, trauma, and the impact of Batman's long-standing battle with the Joker on himself and Gotham City. It also examines the psychological toll of their ongoing conflict. 4. **Artwork**: Jason Fabok's artwork is highly praised for its detailed and cinematic style, which complements Geoff Johns' storytelling. 5. **Reception**: "Batman: Three Jokers" received generally positive reviews for its exploration of the Joker-Batman dynamic and its emotional depth. It was seen as a significant addition to Batman lore, providing new insights into the characters involved. Overall, "Batman: Three Jokers" is a compelling exploration of one of comic book's most iconic rivalries, offering fans a fresh perspective on the Joker and his relationship with the Dark Knight.
